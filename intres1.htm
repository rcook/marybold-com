Practice: Accessing Archived Documents

Follow this link to the U.S. Census Bureau to locate one or more PDF files; open them on your computer system simply by following on-screen instructions.

http://www.census.gov

PDF stands for portable document format; .pdf is the filename extension. PDF files can be opened by Adobe Acrobat Reader, a common accessory on today’s computers. The great benefit of PDF is that a page appears on the computer screen (and on the hard copy print-out) exactly as it was created to appear as a standard book or journal page. Typesetting and illustrations are static, which contrasts to the variable output of HTML pages. (HTML, or hypertext markup language, is the format of most web pages on the Internet.)

Browsing Dissertations Online

The most economical way to order dissertations is through a university library since many libraries will purchase any theses requested by faculty and graduate students. But you can also purchase theses yourself through UMI. The website below permits browsing of graduate student works from the past two years. Some entries provide only an abstract; others provide the first 24 pages of the work for on-screen viewing or print-out.

UMI homepage:

  http://www.umi.com/

Direct to the dissertation service called ProQuest:

http://wwwlib.umi.com/dissertations/

Summer 1999, UMI was purchased by Bell & Howell. So far, this has not affected the location of the website or publishing standards of theses and dissertations. If these URLs do not work, however, you may need to track down a new site through Bell & Howell.

Statistics

University resources for the study and use of statistics:

University of Vermont—David Howell offers detailed help on many topics. For fun and erudition, click through these options: Stats—Lecture and lab ideas—Chi-Square—three door data—the Monte Hall question—follow link to Click and Clack (the Tappit brothers) cartalk.com. You will find a clever interactive tutorial on probability.

http://www.uvm.edu/~dhowell/StatPages/StatHomePage.html

Claremont College website WISE

http://acad.cgu.edu/wise/

Government sites that publish quick-to-access statistics:

Census Bureau “brief” stats

http://www.census.gov/statab/www/brief.htm

National Center of Education Statistics—For topical statistics worth scanning, click on Fast Facts in the left column. Click on your browser’s reload or refresh button to read new questions, or just click on an education level below for a list of all pertinent questions.

http://nces.ed.gov/
