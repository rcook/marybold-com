Enhance your résumé "Relates well to people" is an expected qualification in the family field. We all relate well to people. That's the norm, the baseline. So, your résumé must emphasize the things you can do beyond "relating" to people.

What do you do that everyone else in FS doesn't?

Have a working knowledge of all major statistical packages

Demonstrate expertise in computer software (Microsoft Office, web page design/authoring, etc.)

Speak publicly, ideally across several age groups

Work with special populations

Earn a certification such as CFLE

Speak multiple languages

Be willing to relocate

Perfect your résumé
Step 1. Determine whether it should be SHORT or LONG.
Step 2. SHORT: Keep it to one page to suit most employers. For examples, review the websites listed at the end of this page.

LONG: Make it look scholarly for an application to higher education. For guidance, review articles at the online version of The Chronicle for Higher Education.

Step 3.

Run spell-check AND ask a friend to proof-read it.
Distribute your résumé Submit one to the career services office of your university.

Send several to your professors, along with a personal note asking each  to keep you in mind when they hear of jobs.

Put one version on your own website, list another version with an online search engine (like http://www.monster.com/), and offer an electronic version in a .doc file or .rtf via e-mail.

Carry several copies with you for distribution to "acquaintances" at meetings and conferences. (Keep in mind, also, that most job leads come from the acquaintances and friends we see only occasionally.)
