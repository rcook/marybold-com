Off-shift work refers to staggered employment schedules so that any time one parent is at work, the other parent is at home. Historically, off-shifting has probably frequently been a feature of parenting when all the adults in the family were employed. In the 1990s, the strategy gained public attention. By some estimates, as many as 20% of two-parents-employed families use off-shifting to allow for parental care of children.

Some possible benefits of off-shifting: children are always cared for by a parent, more extracurricular activities for children are possible, savings in child care costs, increased earnings on the job(s).

Some possible drawbacks of off-shifting: lack of couple time, high stress, incomplete family time, sleep deprivation.
