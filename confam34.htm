Two-parent family:

Having two parents is still considered an ideal family form in American society. Reasons are varied, but one of the reasons is pure dollars and cents. Having two parents typically indicates that the child has adequate economic resources. A child who lives with one parent is substantially more likely to have a family income that falls below the poverty line. (In 1997, a family of four with an annual income below $16,400 was below the Federal poverty line.)

American children living with two parents:

1980: 77%
1998: 68%
