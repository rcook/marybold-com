SOURCE: U.S. Bureau of the Census

Children (< 18) covered by health insurance, 1987-97 

  • In 1997, 85% of children had health insurance coverage. (Fairly stable since 1987)

  • The number of children who had no health insurance at any time during 1997 was 10.7 million, or 15% of all children. 

  • In 1997, 71% of Hispanic children were covered by health insurance, compared to 89% of White children and 81% of Black children. 

  • Young children (0 - 5) are more likely than older children to have public rather than private health insurance. 

  • Public consists primarily of Medicaid, but also includes Medicare and CHAMPUS (health benefit program for the armed forces, being replaced by Tricare).
