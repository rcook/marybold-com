Course Description:

Techniques for analyzing community resources and needs as related to the family life cycle; adaptation of school environment and programs to utilize available resources and contribute to the development of family and community life.  (3 credit hours)

 

 

General Course Objectives:

The student will be able to:

1. Define child development and the family from an ecological perspective.

2. Describe the effects of community support on child and family development.

3. Identify community resources available for children and families with special needs.

4. Identify and analyze societal trends affecting children and families.

5. Identify sources of information and strategies for child and family advocacy and practice advocacy.

6. Identify careers related to child development and family relations.

7. Utilize career strategies including networking.

8. Perform an in-depth study of one community agency or organization through volunteer activity.

9. Identify family-centered practices in agencies and organizations.

 

 

Required Course Readings:

Goffin, S. G., & Lombardi, J. (1988). Speaking out: Early childhood advocacy. Washington D.C.: NAEYC.

Required Activities/Grading:

 

Readings: Read all assignments outlined in this syllabus as well as articles assigned in class. All articles will be accessible online or distributed in class.

 

Group work in class: Small group projects, such as case study reviews, will take place in class. All students are expected to participate fully.

 

Written assignments: All written work is due at the start of class. I have a strong preference for work submitted via e-mail (address to: 4610@marybold.com).

 

Networking Luncheon (20 percent): Participate in the planning and execution of the Annual Networking Luncheon set for October 11, 2000. Plan to be available for the Luncheon from 11 a.m. until 3 p.m. on that date. Assist in the writing of a committee report (due date: October 18) that includes recommendations for future Luncheons. Write a self-report of your participation (due date: October 25) that accurately describes your work using phrases such as I created … I assisted … I co-produced … I took the initiative to ….

 

Mid-term Exam and Final Exam (20 percent).

 

Volunteer Work (20 percent): Perform 15 hours of volunteer work in an approved program providing services to children and families. Secure permissions from Dr. Bold and from the program by September 20. Make a short report on your progress by mid-semester. Recommendation: complete all volunteer hours before Thanksgiving. Write a 500-word report of your experience (due date: December 6).

 

Resource File (20 percent). Collect information on 20 agencies, programs, or organizations that offer services to children and families. Up to 10 of the resources may be Internet-based. Display variety in your choices: social, medical, emotional, educational, protective services, parenting. At least 5 of the resources should be detailed with information concerning their target populations, funding, staffing, and history. All of the resources should include name, physical address, mailing address, URL, phone, e-mail, and brief description of services or programs. These resource files MUST be submitted via e-mail. A standard format will be designed in class; please use only that format. Submit at least 5 sources by October 18 (mid-semester). Due date for all resources is November 29.

 

Advocacy and Media Project (20 percent): Create a log of 5 – 10 advocacy issues (related to children and family) observed in the media. You may choose to focus on how the issues are treated in campaigns for the fall elections. Share your log in group work in class (starting mid-September) and be prepared to show it to Dr. Bold at least once before mid-semester. Select one of the issues and develop a 500-word position paper on it (due date: November 15). Write a letter of advocacy on this issue for mailing to a legislative representative. Be prepared to share your issue, position paper, and letter in class. Present your letter to Dr. Bold before mailing (due date: November 29).
