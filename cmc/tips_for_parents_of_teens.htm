Tips for Parents of Teens

1. Create a code word for the subject line in case teen uses an unfamiliar  friend's computer to send messages to you.

2. To increase family time, place computers in a family room or living room where the TV or entertainment center is.

3. Encourage college students to set up their computer in a shared family space when at home to avoid isolation.

4. Encourage teenagers to establish a traditional e-mail name that will go on college and job applications.

5. When communicating with a child living away from home, these tips might help:

  • Keep messages short.

  • E-mail frequently.

  • Make messages "non-demanding."

  • Give information; do not ask for it.

  • Encourage your spouse to e-mail directly.

  • For non-custodial parents: use e-mail to create/maintain open communication with your ex-spouse. Use lots of cc: lines for shared messages, especially those concerning dates and times. Resist the urge to use blind cc: lines. Keep in mind that e-mail messages "sound" curt, so build in polite conventions such as "Regards," or "Warm regards," or a similar closing.
