“Co-parent” commonly has two meanings currently:

(1) Shared parenting responsibility by divorced parents and their current spouses. For example, a child may be reared jointly by mother and step-father, and by father and step-mother. Regardless of the child’s residential status, the parents may describe themselves as co-parents.

(2) Parenting responsibility shared by a same-sex couple in residence with their child. (See also GLBT family.) Research has reported that the use of the term “co-parent” is sometimes a parent’s way of obliquely communicating to teachers that the parents are gay or lesbian (Wickens, 1993).
