 Aversion Therapy

Aversion therapy, or a punishment like requiring a child to smoke a pack of cigarettes, is not recommended. While some children respond well to a more minor "aversion" punishment (example below), anything involving a substance like tobacco or alcohol runs health risks. Even if a health risk is contained, some children will respond with defiance, making the punishment ineffective anyway.

As a parent, I used an aversion punishment exactly once. My 9-year-old son had been lighting matches on the back porch. I required him to stand at the kitchen sink and light (and douse) every match in a large box. He was first offended, and then miserably bored. That one punishment worked for him. 

In retrospect, I can see that it could have backfired. I was lucky.

Aversion may work for some kids and some issues. But the risk of an undesired response is great. As a rule, parents are wise to forego it.
