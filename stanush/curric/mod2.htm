Introduction to Family Dynamic

We're going to form a blended family here. I need 7 volunteers who are willing to be actors for us today: 4 women and 3 men. I'll tell you a little about the family members and warn you that the volunteers will be role-playing for us. 

We'll need women to play the mother, her 17-year-old step-daughter, her 13-year-old daughter, and her 9-year-old step-daughter.

Men actors will play the father, his 16-year-old step-son, and his 4-year-old step-son. We'll ask the 4-year-old to sit in a chair so that we can remember that he's a preschooler.

Volunteers? Here are name cards to help you remember who you are!

[If students offer to create a same-sex partnership, ask that this scenario be played straight. The role-play of a homosexual couple sometimes generates repeated laughter in an adolescent group; it simply slows down the curriculum too much.]

Transparency 2a

Thank you. I'll ask the parents to come up first. Welcome, Terry. You'll see from your card that you are "sensitive to others' needs, but easily worried." And if Marcus will come forward, we will describe your Starbucks romance. Marcus is ultra-logical about everything, but had a passion for golf. When asked, he can listen patiently.

Terry was divorced 2 years ago when her ex-husband Tad called her one Friday night to announce that he and his secretary were in Kansas City and not coming back any time soon. Terry had not worked up to that point, but she quickly entered the work force in order to support herself and her kids.

Marcus is a widower. His wife Caroline died 4 years ago in a car accident. He and his daughters grieved for several years but they have begun to move forward now.

Terry and Marcus met at a Starbucks. Both had been out shopping for groceries for their homes and stopped by Starbucks for a cappuccino. They chatted briefly at the counter while waiting for their orders.

The next Friday evening, Terry and Marcus just happened to bump into each other at exactly the same time at Starbucks. After several such meetings, they decided to go out to dinner together, and from there the relationship grew.

So, months later, they decide to marry. We will eavesdrop as they gather in their first family meeting to discuss the blending of the two families.

Terry, will you call your kids up to stand next to you? Their names are Tod, Tammy, and Timmy.

Tod is a talented guy. He plans to be a film director. But at 16 he's required to still be in high school and that frustrates him. He's heard the story of Steven Spielberg as a teenager. Spielberg walked out of high school one day and onto a movie lot. He took over an empty trailer and started holding meetings with writers and producers. It took 2 or 3 weeks for the movie studio to catch on that a teenager was on the lot. They kicked him out, and he had to go back to high school. Tod likes that story and he lives for his own exit from high school. His goal is to get to UCLA Film School.

Tammy is 13. She's an athlete. Plays on every team at her junior high. Very active, very assertive. Forget those things people say about middle children. Tammy is not overlooked, and certainly not quiet.

Timmy, you'll be sitting through this to keep you nice and short. Timmy's only 4 years old. He may not have a lot of skills yet, but he is an expert at Pokemon.

Marcus, will you please call your daughters forward?

Martina-Gabriele is 17 and a senior in high school. She is an average student, but super-responsible. If she makes a commitment, she sticks to it. She was not happy to hear the news of her father dating Terry and she was surprised when he announced the marriage. But she will support her Dad, so she's going to try to get along with Terry.

Christine-Angelena is not so supportive. She is 9 and quite aware that with 4-year-old Timmy around, she has lost her status of "baby of the family."

Family Negotiation of Bedrooms

Now, let's see how this blended family handles its first task: sorting out the bedrooms. Terry's house is larger than Marcus's, so they have decided to live there. There are 4 bedrooms plus a small study that can be used as a bedroom. Let's eavesdrop as we listen to how this family sorts out the rooms. Terry and Marcus, how will you present this issue to the kids?

[The family members negotiate the issue. When the talking winds down, take over the facilitation again.]

So, you believe you have a solution. Audience, any comments for this group?

Discipline Scenario

Well, you handled that very well. Obviously, you need a tougher problem. Here's a scenario for parents, Tod, Christine-Angelena, and Timmy. Martina and Tammy, you can sit down for a while.

OK. New scenario: Terry and Marcus are at work. It's after-school time. Tod is at the house because he is supposed to get home quickly every day to receive Christine-Angelena and Timmy. They are dropped off by a school bus.

Today, Tod has friends visiting. They are sitting out on the patio, smoking cigarettes. Tod does not smoke, but some of his friends do.

Christine-Angelena and Timmy arrive home and go straight out to the patio where the teenagers are sitting. Christine asks one of the teenagers for a cigarette. He gives it to her and she puffs and coughs at length.

Just as this is happening, Terry arrives home. She sees this scene from the kitchen window and heads straight out to the group.

Let's eavesdrop. We want to observe how Terry handles this situation.

[Terry can take a variety of paths. Depending on her actions, these subjects may be raised either by the actors or the audience: disciplining in front of a kid's friends, aversion therapy as punishment, immediate discipline vs waiting for other parent, disciplining by a step-parent, what effect these scenes have on the 4-year-old.]

Now, let's bring Marcus home. Who is likely to tell Marcus what's going on?

[Marcus's reaction varies from class to class. An important factor is the dynamic between Marcus and Terry. These subjects may arise: parents' agreement about punishment, father's role as disciplinarian, age-appropriate punishments, education vs punishment, corporal punishment or spanking.]

Let's get a sense of the audience's reaction to these parents' handling of the situation. Does someone want to comment? Show of hands for agreement with that?

[Option: Let's try another scenario. This one is for Tammy, Martina-Gabriele, and the parents. Everyone else can sit down. Martina-Gabriele has told her parents that she's going to a party with friends on Friday night. What she has not told them is that the party is at a fraternity house. But Tammy knows. Tammy sometimes lifts the receiver of an extension phone when Martina is talking with friends. So, Tammy decides to tell Terry and Marcus. Family, how do you want to play this out?

Depending on the actors' choices, these topics may arise: step-sibling rivalry, parents' reactions to lies or withholding of facts, limits on teenagers' activities, dynamic between step-parent and step-child.]
