 Accordion Effect

Within blended families, accordion effect refers to the household changes that occur when step-siblings arrive and depart weekend or holiday visits. The size of household changes as well as the family dynamic.

Depending on available space, children may be asked to share rooms and even beds. All other resources are brought into play, from a parent's time to use of the video game set. Some territorial matters may be obvious while others are known only among the children. Too, these matters may vary dramatically from one child to the next. One child may care deeply about a disrupted schedule; another may be offended by a stepsibling drinking the last of the orange juice.

Parent-child relations can be greatly affected, especially if a parent asks resident children to "give" in order to preserve peace with a visiting child. Even when adults see such a strategy as appropriate, children may struggle with its inherent unfairness. When the adults do not agree about the matter, added stress is created.
