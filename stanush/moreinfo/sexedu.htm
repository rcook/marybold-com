 More Information about Sexuality Education

The following information is not typically shared with students in the classroom:

SIECUS. The Sexuality Information and Education Council of the U.S. (SIECUS) is a national, nonprofit organization which affirms that sexuality is a natural and healthy part of living.  Incorporated in 1964, SIECUS develops, collects, and disseminates information, promotes comprehensive education about sexuality, and advocates the right of individuals to make responsible sexual choices. http://www.siecus.org/index.html

Teen Health. MedlinePlus Health Information is a review of current publications drawn from Medline. All are published by the National Library of Medicine. The NLM website is a user-friendly gateway to medical literature. To go directly to Teen Health: http://www.nlm.nih.gov/medlineplus/teenhealth.html

U.S. National Library of Medicine (NLM). http://www.nlm.nih.gov/

Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC). http://www.cdc.gov/

Condoms. Reliability of condoms is a popular topic on the Internet. Ratings are available, but the most common reports are from 1993, 1995, and 1998. As some of the reports admit, the highest rated brands "are no longer manufactured." Although the question of condom ratings is not properly answered on the Internet, the discussion of ratings serves as powerful stimulus for adolescents to seek more information about contraceptives. Across all brands, these statistics are commonly reported regarding effectiveness in preventing pregnancy: 

If used perfectly with no errors, effectiveness is at least 98%.

If used typically with some errors (meaning as condoms are typically used in the U.S.), effectiveness is 88%.
