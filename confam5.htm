Research into people’s definitions of family tells us that although we all agree on using the term, we don’t agree on what it means. How many people form a family? One study reported a range of 0 to 40 members (Trost, 1990). 

What boundaries frame family? Some people call parents and children a family. Others wouldn’t think to draw such a small circle, but rather extend the boundary to include cousins and more. For some, the boundary includes animals. For some, an extension is made for deceased relatives.

Family may refer to blood relations, but may also mean fictive kin—sometimes to the exclusion of blood relatives. In fact, biological criteria rarely form the basis for naming family. Neither do legal or residential descriptions (Jorgenson, 1989). Family is more frequently described as the system of relations that develops from everyday contact (Jorgenson, 1989) and is reinforced by the members’ gathering for celebrations and rituals. Behavior and communication, then, may better indicate family membership.

Does this mean family can mean anything? The researchers confirm this: the definition of family is highly subjective and boundaries of family are drawn very differently.

Since composition is so varied, it is hard to define family, and impossible to depict a typical one. In the family field, few experts try to define it, but continue to rely on the word—to refer to any set or system of relations calling itself a family.
