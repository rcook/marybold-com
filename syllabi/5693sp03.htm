Course Description: 

 

Development of foundational knowledge of family sciences research methodology; emphasis on skill in planning and conducting family sciences research projects. Three lecture hours a week. Credit: Three hours. 

 

State Standards and Professional Guidelines Alignment:

In this course, emphasis will be placed on the following domains and ExCET competency: Competency 012 – Ethical, Legal, and Professional Standards. You may find more complete information on the ExCET competencies at the following websites: http://www.sbec.state.tx.us/ and http://www.excet.nesinc.com .

Course Objectives: Students will be able to:

  1. Demonstrate an understanding of the major ethical issues in research.
  2. Demonstrate an understanding of the scientific method.
  3. Differentiate between theoretical and non-theoretical research.
  4. Define independent, dependent, and extraneous variables.
  5. Differentiate between operational and non-operational definitions.
  6. Differentiate between the different kinds of research.
  7. Discern the results or research, differentiating between findings and conclusions.

 

Course Requirements:

 

1.      Portal Account. Create and use a TWU Pioneer Portal e-mail account for use in online courses. If your e-mail to Bold reflects a Portal account, you will be credited 5 points.

2.      Self Introduction. Use the Personal Information and Personal Homepage functions of FS 5843 Blackboard to introduce yourself to the class. (You may use previously prepared materials as long as they are updated for this semester.) 5 points.

3.      Introduction to Research Terms. Using resource material from FS 5221 (on the FS 5693 CD-ROM), select 15 terms to define. Submit your definitions in an “.rtf” file in the Exchange File of your “individual group.” (Instructions are available in the Blackboard course space.) Students who have taken FS 5221 are not required to submit this report. Due date: February 6. 5 points.

4.      Research Design Teams. Contribute to a team working on design and execution of a short-term research project, collecting data within our class. Sample Team titles: Project Conceptualization, Instrument, IRB Application, Literature Review, Data Collection, Data Analysis, Conclusions & Implications, Report Writing. 20 points.

5.      Babbie Boards. Contribute to the discussion of the textbook in Blackboard. There will be five “Babbie Boards” covering the five main divisions of the book. 15 points.

6.      Article Abstracts. Select three scholarly journal articles from the research area of your choice and abstract according to the guides on the CD-ROM. Select at least one quantitative study and at least one qualitative study. In your first abstract, you may limit your work to reporting on the sample and the methodology. Submit your abstracts in your individual group space on BB. Submit one in February, one in March, and one in April. 15 points.

7.      APA Drill. Starting in February, Bold will post an APA tip or pointer on each weekday. Optional quizzes in Blackboard will be posted each weekend to review the previous week’s information about using the APA Publication Manual. While not required for a grade in this course, the quizzes will serve you in making APA style standards easier to understand and easier to use, in this course as well as any other grad class that requires APA references and writing standards.

8.      Ethics Paper. Write a short paper on a research ethics issue in your field. Length: 600 words minimum. References: 6 minimum. Follow APA style. Set your own due date for this paper anytime prior to April 10. Notify Bold of the due date by February 6. 15 points.

9.      Final Exam. This exam will be online, with a preview copy available for study. Practice tests will be posted to Blackboard starting three weeks before the final. These can be taken as many times as you like in order to become familiar with the testing environment. 20 points.

10.  Transition Semester: This semester of FS 5693 marks a transition from a course that required a professional paper to satisfy the writing requirement for the Master’s degree to a course that supports Portfolio requirements of the four components of the Department: Counseling & Development, Family Studies, Family Therapy, and Early Childhood Development & Education. This year is also the first year that the Department no longer requires completion of FS 5221, which focused on reading and understanding research. To support students’ and faculty members’ transition to the new Portfolio requirements, the FS 5693 schedule will include two weeks during which no Blackboard assignments are due. You may want to use this time to work on projects suitable to the Portfolio. Sometime during the semester, you may also want consult with your advisor about how your course work relates to your Master’s Portfolio. (As an example, the Ethics Paper for this course might be appropriate for inclusion in your Portfolio, either as a stand-alone paper or as materialAAAAbe incorporated in another Portfolio element.) Your advisor should not be asked to review your work for this course or edit any papers for you. Also, FS 5693 does not require that you consult with your advisor. 

 

Policies and Procedures:

 

  1. The style manual for the American Psychological Association (APA), 5th ed., should be used for citations, references, and manuscript style. Internet citations may follow current APA format or National Library of Medicine format; if using NLM, select the option of authors’ initials rather than first and middle names. Another format for Internet citation may be appropriate; please notify Bold of the reason for your choice and then use it consistently. Advisory: Do not assume that Internet citation style used in this course will automatically be acceptable to a thesis/dissertation committee or a journal editor.
  2. Work should be turned in according to due dates. Grade deductions of 5% per day will be made for late work.
  3. Participation in all class activities is expected.
