Course Description:

EDCD 4140 explores the components of before- and after-school child care and related programs that are developmentally appropriate and benefit children aged 5 through 12. Components include planning, implementing, and evaluating activities, facilities, programs, staff, budgets, and other aspects necessary for providing healthy services for children.  (3 credit hours)

 

 

General Course Objectives:

 

  1. Use and promote interpersonal problem-solving skills that can be taught to and caught by school-age children.
  2. Design programs that will enhance the emotional, social, behavioral, mental, and physical health of children.
  3. Apply basic principles learned previously in child development classes.
  4. Become familiar with local, city, county, state, and federal mandates that relate to the development of facilities and programs for school-age groups.
  5. Explore the laws, rules, and regulations related to caring for these children.
  6. Explore the liability, insurance needs, financing requirements, staff needs, and other factors of operating these types of programs.
  7. Explore the need for family, community, and school support for the before- and after-school care of children aged 5 through 12.
  8. Explore the needs of parents for school-aged child care during the vacations of their children.
  9. Explore the rules and regulations of operating before- and after-school programs in public school buildings.
  10. Explore other types and needs for programs that would serve this age group.
  11. Explore how to plan, implement, and evaluate child care programs for children aged 5 through 12.
  12. Learn appropriate discipline techniques, communication patterns, and staff training strategies.

Required Activities/Grading:

 

Mini-lecture (10 percent): Prepare a short lecture on some aspect of child development. In class on January 23, work as a group to outline lecture topics and make assignments. Bumgarner may be used as a guide to topics, but other ideas are welcome. All lectures should be scheduled for February 6 and 13. Allow time on the 13th for Bold to present on GS Theory and for the class to discuss gender socialization.

 

Field Trip (10 percent): Working in a group, plan a field trip for this class. Deadline for schedule is January 30. Try to use the dates of March 20 and April 10 for the actual visiting days. Components of this assignment: select a destination, arrange for our class to visit, prepare map/directions for the class, conduct the class at the field trip site, write thank-you letter to hosting facility. Possibilities: Children’s Courtyard developmentally-appropriate facility/program for school-age child care, YMCA, inner-city setting vs suburbia, programs for special populations.

GS Reaction Paper (5 percent): 500-word essay on your reaction to GS Theory as discussed in class on February 13. No references required. If submitted by paper, APA format is required. If submitted by e-mail, APA format is not required. Bold prefers e-mail submission. If you do not know how to attach documents in e-mail, use this assignment as your chance to learn how. All essays will be checked for length. Letter grade of A is not possible if essay is shorter than 500 words.

 

Issue Reaction Paper (5 percent): 500-word essay on your reaction to an issue discussed in class on Febuary 20. No references required. If submitted by paper, APA format is required. If submitted by e-mail, APA format is not required. Bold prefers e-mail submission. All essays will be checked for length. Letter grade of A is not possible if essay is shorter than 500 words.

 

Teen Reaction Paper (5 percent): 500-word essay on your reaction to an issue discussed in class on April 17. No references required. If submitted by paper, APA format is required. If submitted by e-mail, APA format is not required. Bold prefers e-mail submission. All essays will be checked for length. Letter grade of A is not possible if essay is shorter than 500 words.

 

Game/activity (5 percent): In class on April 3, lead the class in a game or activity appropriate to school-age children. (No group work allowed.)

 

Design Project (20 percent): This project will be discussed in class to allow some customization of the assignment according to your background and career plans. Select your own due date and communicate it to Bold. No projects will be accepted after May 1.

 

Midterm exam (20 percent): You are welcome to submit questions for the mid-term exam. Submit via e-mail to 4140@marybold.com .

 

Final exam (20 percent): You are welcome to submit questions for the mid-term exam. Submit via e-mail to 4140@marybold.com .

 

 

A = 90%, B = 80%, C = 70%, D = 60%

 

 

Assignments are due at the beginning of class periods. E-mail submissions must be received on the date listed in the syllabus. No allowance is made for downed e-mail servers; either work ahead or have a back-up e-mail account to allow for timely submission. Papers should be typed, double-spaced; attachments are preferred for e-mail submissions. Five percent penalty will be assessed for each day (not class) an assignment is late.
