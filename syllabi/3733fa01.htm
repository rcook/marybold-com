Catalog Description:

Organization, planning, and implementation of family life and consumer education programs. Emphasis placed on curriculum, methods of delivery, resources, evaluation, and professional ethics. Three lecture hours a week. Credit: Three hours.

 

 

Course Objectives:

  1. Design instruction for family and consumer sciences that promotes critical thinking skills and addresses a variety of learning styles.
  2. Utilize technology to promote learning and expand instruction options.
  3. Determine concepts, objectives, content, and instructional strategies relevant to family and consumer sciences.
  4. Develop instructional techniques based on curriculum goals and learner characteristics.
  5. Demonstrate varied teaching methods and technologies.
  6. Design appropriate evaluation strategies for family and consumer sciences.
  7. Evaluation professional and educational programs.
  8. Demonstrate professional competence and commitment.
  9. Formulate public relations strategies.
  10. Evaluate teaching/learning resources.
  11. Practice public speaking.

 

 

Course Requirements:

 

Mid-term and final exams. Short-answer and objective questions will be drawn from class lectures, class activities, and the required texts. The tests are designed to allow students to demonstrate their knowledge, not to trip up students with trick questions.

 

Participate in the creation of evaluation standards and the semester schedule for this course: Our study includes the important topics of planning and evaluating. Thus, we will work as a group in the first weeks of the semester to create a standard by which assignments will be graded as well as blocking out the semester for reading assignments and due dates. Because Bold is a strong believer in formative evaluation, revisions will be allowed if the class decides as a group that initial decisions were unwise.

 

Report on adult learners: Write a report that incorporates these topics: characteristics of adult learners, needs/interests of adult learners, motivation of adult learners, appropriate approaches to instructing adults. Cite at least three sources located through the Internet. Minimum length: 750 words.

 

POP-UP (30-second) presentations: On class dates of your choosing, make five (5) presentations appropriate for family life education. Recommended time length: 30 seconds (50 - 80 words, typically). Absolute time limit: 60 seconds (grade of F if you speak longer than one minute). You are absolutely forbidden to spend any money on these presentations! Typically, these will be presented at the start of each class period. If you anticipate working in community (or you already do), use these presentations to create polished projects you can use in the future with a specific audience.

 

Professional journal review: As a class, we will review journals that are utilized in the family and consumer sciences. We will analyze one research article to understand how practitioners make use of research literature. To assist in reading research, review the following partial website on statistics: ROPER Online Learning, Module #3, Block 4, Introduction to Statistics [Available: www.unt.edu/cpe/module3/blk4.htm]. At that website, follow all the hyperlinks to extra information, follow the hyperlinks to the No-Pressure Homework, follow all the links to “Next Page.” Finally, follow the Next Page link to the Self-Assessment. Answer questions 21 – 30 (the only ones relating to Block 4, Introduction to Statistics) and be prepared to share your answers in class.

 

Statement of career plans or professional philosophy: Definition or description of career goals and/or your professional philosophy. Appropriate comments (but not necessarily required) may be: your personal beliefs about your work, your professional goals for the future, your outlook on populations typically served by your profession. Minimum length: 250 words.

 

Adult education class: Attend and evaluate a seminar or class that addresses adult learners who are not receiving college credit for their attendance. Report on these aspects of the experience: program or content, effectiveness in achieving goals for the intended audience, appeal to adult learners, evaluation of the instructional techniques. Minimum length: 500 words.

 

4-part Plan: Plan a 2-hour adult education seminar. 1) Prepare a detailed outline of the seminar, including logistics for it. This can be accomplished in two pages, typed. 2) Prepare a single piece of publicity for it. 3) Develop an evaluation instrument that could be used to assess the effectiveness of your seminar. 4) Present a 10-minute excerpt of the seminar for the class.

 

Grading: Weight of assignments will be discussed in class. Final grades follow a standard scale:

A = 90%, B = 80%, C = 70%, D = 60%

 

Policies and Procedures:

  1. Written work should be typed. The style manual for the American Psychological Association (APA), 5th ed., should be used for citations, references, and manuscript style. APA format is not required for e-mail submissions.
  2. Written work should be turned in according to schedule. Grade deductions will be made for late work. Five percent penalty will be assessed for each day (not class) an assignment is late. Work past one week late will not be accepted.
  3. I welcome e-mail submissions. Attachments should be in Microsoft Word. I acknowledge every assignment with a message that “XXX was received in good order.” If you submit an assignment and do not receive this reply within a few days, you should assume that I did not receive your assignment. Due dates are firm regardless of server problems. Assignments are due at the beginning of class periods or, if submitted via e-mail, on the due date. No allowance is made for downed e-mail servers; either work ahead or have a back-up e-mail account to allow for timely submission. Papers should be typed, double-spaced; attachments are preferred for e-mail submissions. My preference for virtually all work is submission via e-mail.
  4. Attendance is expected. Be aware that test material is drawn heavily from class lectures and activities. Other aspects of evaluation (such as directions for papers and presentations) are covered in detail during class and only summarily in print. High level of attendance will be considered if a grade is borderline (1 point from the next letter grade).

Family Sciences Diversity Policy:

 

The concept of diversity implies a total commitment to an open context that promotes equality, social tolerance, and the right to express different points of view. The Family Sciences Department practices and celebrates diversity and creates a culture characterized by a climate of inclusion rather than exclusion, where people are involved in working together for a common good. Diversity refers to the inclusion of all persons regardless of their differences. These differences include but are not limited to ethnicity, race, culture, gender, age, socio-economic status, religion, sexual orientation, and physical disability. The Family Sciences Department does not condone discrimination in any form and complies with Texas Woman’s University Non-Discrimination Policy. The faculty actively seek enrichment through the strength, power, and wisdom of diversity.
