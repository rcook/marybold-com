Catalog Description:

FS 5623. Investigation of occupational trends and theories of occupational choice. Emphasis on implications of career choice for the economy, society, family, and the individual. Three lecture hours a week. Credit: Three hours.

 

Course Requirements:

1. Class attendance and participation. (Absences exceeding four (4) hours makes an A not possible.) Attend all classes and participate in discussions and activities. Attendance and participation at on-campus meetings = 10% of semester grade.

2. Contribute to a Theory Timeline that traces the development of theories in this field in this centurty. Timeline submissions = 10% of semester grade.

3. Lead discussion of a major topic from Zunker. (See chapter options in Course Calendar at end of this syllabus.) Leadership of major topic = 20% of semester grade.

4. Lead discussion of a minor topic from Zunker, of your choosing. (Sign up for a date on the Course Calendar and announce your topic and Zunker page numbers by the end of the first week of the Maymester. Leadership of minor topic = 10% of semester grade.

5. Contribute to a web sitelet called Careers in the Family Field (working title). In class we will outline topics for the sitelet; submissions may be in .htm or .txt files. Website submissions = 30% of semester grade.

6. Final exam = 20% of semester grade.

Family Sciences Diversity Policy:

 

The concept of diversity implies a total commitment to an open context that promotes equality, social tolerance, and the right to express different points of view. The Family Sciences Department practices and celebrates diversity and creates a culture characterized by a climate of inclusion rather than exclusion, where people are involved in working together for a common good. Diversity refers to the inclusion of all persons regardless of their differences. These differences include but are not limited to ethnicity, race, culture, gender, age, socio-economic status, religion, sexual orientation, and physical disability. The Family Sciences Department does not condone discrimination in any form and complies with Texas Woman’s University Non-Discrimination Policy. The faculty actively seek enrichment through the strength, power, and wisdom of diversity.
