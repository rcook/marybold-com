Catalog Description:

Current research and issues in family sciences: emphasis on conceptual and theoretical frameworks in guiding research design and critical analysis of recent literature in family sciences. Three lecture hours a week. Credit: Three hours.

 

 

Course Objectives:

  1. Identify major conceptual frameworks and empirical techniques prevalent in studying families.
  2. Identify and critique selected theoretical and conceptual literature on selected family issues.
  3. Become a knowledgeable consumer of conceptual and empirical literature on families.
  4. Demonstrate skills in evaluating, synthesizing, and summarizing.
  5. Demonstrate in-depth understanding of selected theoretical and conceptual perspectives.

 

Course Requirements:

  1. Portal Account. Create and use a TWU Pioneer Portal e-mail account for correspondence with Bold about this class.
  2. Weekend #1 in class: Content analysis of a journal. Working in a group of 3 or 4 members, select a scholarly journal that has published at least four issues across 2001 and 2002. Select four consecutive issues from those years and analyze all articles and essays for conceptual or theoretical frameworks and for empirical approaches or techniques. Prepare a grid for display in class (a sample will be provided). 15% of grade. (No partial credit)
  3. Weekend #2 in class: Test on Phelps, Furstenberg, Colby. 10% of grade.
  4. Beginning Weekend #2 in class: Serve as the discussant for assigned readings from the DIR. Update the research in the years since 2000, and especially consider the use of longitudinal research. You can assume that everyone will have read the material in DIR before class but will not necessarily be familiar with your update material. 15% of grade.
  5. Submit by Weekend #3: Contribute 500 to 1000 words of scrupulously clean research reporting to a class website on current longitudinal research. The site and topics will be conceptualized in the first two weekends. 10% of grade.
  6. Due Weekend #4 in class, a report on current research: Option 1: Attend the annual conference of TCFR at TWU on April 3 & 4 and report orally in class on the issues featured at that conference. This assignment will require pre-conference research into the announced sessions. Be prepared to evaluate the usefulness of the conference sessions in light of your research into the issues. Option 2: Written review of recent research on an approved topic. Use APA 5th edition as your style guide. Text of manuscript should be minimum 2000 words; reference list of at least 15 sources will extend beyond this length. Topic must be submitted on or before February 7 in class. Be prepared to orally present an overview of your topic during Weekend #4. Either option: 30% of grade.
  7. Weekend #4 in class: Final exam. To be conceptualized in class. 20% of grade.

 

Policies and Procedures:

  1. The style manual for the American Psychological Association (APA), 5th ed., should be used for citations, references, and manuscript style. Internet citations may follow current APA format or National Library of Medicine format; if using NLM, select the option of authors’ initials rather than first and middle names. Another format for Internet citation may be appropriate; please notify Bold of the reason for your choice and then use it consistently. Advisory: Do not assume that Internet citation style used in this course will automatically be acceptable to a thesis/dissertation committee or a journal editor.
  2. Work should be turned in according to due dates. Grade deductions of 5% per day will be made for late work.
  3. Participation in all class activities is expected.

Family Sciences Diversity Policy:

 

The concept of diversity implies a total commitment to an open context that promotes equality, social tolerance, and the right to express different points of view. The Family Sciences Department practices and celebrates diversity and creates a culture characterized by a climate of inclusion rather than exclusion, where people are involved in working together for a common good. Diversity refers to the inclusion of all persons regardless of their differences. These differences include but are not limited to ethnicity, race, culture, gender, age, socio-economic status, religion, sexual orientation, and physical disability. The Family Sciences Department does not condone discrimination in any form and complies with Texas Woman’s University Non-Discrimination Policy. The faculty actively seek enrichment through the strength, power, and wisdom of diversity.
