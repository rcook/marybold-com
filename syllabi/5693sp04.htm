Course Objectives: Students will be able to:

  1. Demonstrate an understanding of the major ethical issues in research.
  2. Demonstrate an understanding of the scientific method.
  3. Differentiate between theoretical and non-theoretical research.
  4. Define independent, dependent, and extraneous variables.
  5. Differentiate between operational and non-operational definitions.
  6. Differentiate between the different kinds of research.
  7. Discern the results or research, differentiating between findings and conclusions.

Policies and Procedures:

 

The style manual for the American Psychological Association (APA), 5th ed., should be used for citations, references, and manuscript style. Written work should be turned in according to class schedule. Grade deductions will be made for late work. Due dates are firm regardless of server problems. Papers should be posted to your File Exchange space on BB. (These servers are more stable and more private than e-mail servers.)

 

Written work, whether in Blackboard or a paper or a test, must display an understanding of the topic or question. Your responses must “fit” the question. If you invent a new question or take your answer down an alternative path, I must assume that you do not know the answer to the question posed. For grading purposes, this would equate to turning in a blank sheet.

 

All written work must include citation of sources. If a passage does not cite a source, I assume that you are presenting the passage as your own writing. When composing your assignment posts and papers, take care that you have not used another author’s work. In scholarly writing, even paraphrasing another’s work calls for a citation so that the idea is credited to the author. Direct quotes must always be identified with quotation marks and appropriate citation (APA-5th edition). Know the principles of copyright protection and plagiarism and write accordingly to meet requirements specified in the TWU Student Handbook, the APA Publication Manual, and recognized standards of scholarly research and writing.

 

Participation in Blackboard discussion is encouraged and expected. Students have editing and removal power over their own posts. I rarely have cause to remove a post. If I read a seriously flawed assignment post, I e-mail the student directly. (No news is good news.) If a minor error exists, I tactfully reply to the post with a suggestion.

 

Incompletes should be rarely needed in my graduate classes. I expect graduate students to complete the requirements of a course in the semester specified or drop the course and re-take it later. (After all, everyone would benefit from adding the semester break to finish a paper or project….)
