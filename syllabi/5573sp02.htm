Catalog Description:

FS 5573. Family Crises. Unique stresses on American family systems emanating from such situations as alcoholism, chronic, physical illness, death, delinquency, drug dependence, mental illness/mental retardation, structure change, and mobility. Three lecture hours a week. Credit: Three hours.

 

 

Course Objectives:

  1. Become familiar with the scholarly literature in the fields of family crisis and resilience.
  2. Understand vocabulary of these fields; be able to use the terminology accurately in writing.
  3. Understand crisis theories and models.
  4. Explore community resources available to families in crisis; identify issues facing professionals who work with families in crisis.
  5. View crisis and resilience through developmental, lifecourse and ecological perspectives with attention to normative events, non-normative events, coping strategies, and adaptation.

 

 

Course Requirements:

  1. Class attendance and participation. Attend all classes and participate in discussions and activities. Absences exceeding a total of four (4) hours from on-campus sessions: semester grade of A will not be possible.
  2. Weekly contribution to a web sitelet geared to undergraduate students. Study the graduate student site at http://twu.edu/~f_bold/divorce/divorce as example. Submissions will be strictly limited to 175 - 225 words (not including references). Research and writing should be scrupulously clean. Cite a variety of references (including Boss). Weekly contributions = 25% of semester grade.
  3. Research-based essay on a topic that incorporates course materials and your professional interests. Minimum length of 1000 words; maximum length of 1250 words; references separate. In grading, Bold will check two things first: word count and reference list. If the word count is too long or the reference list has more than 3 errors, the paper will be returned un-graded. Essay = 25% of semester grade.
  4. As a class member, actively participate in each week of Blackboard discussion. (Note: your access to Blackboard is through an Internet browser. Thus, you can make your contributions from home, from a hotel, from an airport….) Participation = 25% of semester grade.
  5. Final exam. To earn a B in this course, exam grade must be B- or better. To earn an A in this course, exam grade must be B+ or better. Final exam = 25% of semester grade.
