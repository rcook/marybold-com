Course Description:

3 hours. The practice and process of family life education and training of professionals in the child development and family field. Curriculum and program development and evaluation. Teaching strategies and professional responsibilities. Prerequisite: EDCD 2010, 3000.

 

 

Course Objectives:

1.                Review characteristics of family life education.

2.                Utilize techniques for assessing family life education needs of individuals and groups.

3.                Review and evaluate family life education program models, materials, student progress, and program effectiveness.

4.                Demonstrate skill in development of scope and sequence and conceptual outline for family life education.

5.                Develop and share family life education materials, ideas, and strategies.

6.                Demonstrate knowledge of the learner and learning in utilizing educational strategies.

7.                Demonstrate knowledge of planning and implementing family life education programs.

8.                Develop and utilize a variety of educational techniques and strategies in presenting family life education.

9.                Discuss professional opportunities and ethical and legal responsibilities of family life educators.

10.             Analyze effects of public policies and laws on families and their implications for family life educators.

11.             Express sensitivity and understanding of diversity in family life and personal values/beliefs and self-understanding as a family life educator.

12.             Demonstrate communications skills and sensitivity to others to enhance educational effectiveness.

13.             Demonstrate sensitivity to community concerns and values and an understanding of the public relations process.

Required Activities

 

Assignments are due at the beginning of class periods. This time requirement applies to all assignments, including those submitted via e-mail. Five percent penalty will be assessed for each day (not class) an assignment is late. Work past one week late will not be accepted.

 

Attendance is expected. For each absence after one, five points will be deducted from the semester point total. Two points will be deducted for each tardy past two. Be aware that test material is drawn heavily from class lectures and activities. Other aspects of evaluation (such as directions for papers and presentations) are covered in detail during class and only summarily in print.

 

Mid-term and final exams. Short-answer and objective questions will be drawn from class lectures, class activities, and the required texts. The tests are designed to allow students to demonstrate their knowledge, not to trip up students with trick questions.

 

Report on adult learners: Write a report that incorporates these topics: characteristics of adult learners, needs/interests of adult learners, motivation of adult learners, appropriate approaches to instructing adults. Cite at least three sources located through the Internet.

 

30-second presentations: On class dates of your choosing, make five (5) presentations appropriate for family life education. Recommended time length: 30 seconds (roughly 50 words). Absolute time limit: 60 seconds (grade of F if you speak longer than one minute). You are absolutely forbidden to spend any money on these presentations! Typically, these will be presented at the start of each class period. If you anticipate working in community (or you already do), use these presentations to create polished projects you can use in the future with a specific audience.

 

Professional meeting: Attend a meeting of a professional organization or a professional conference. Write a report that incorporates these elements: background information about the organization or conference, details of the meeting (time, location, size or group, etc.), summary of the content of the meeting, evaluation of the meeting. Evaluation should include such things as usefulness (to you personally, to the audience) and suggestions for improvement.

 

Statement of professional philosophy: Definition or description of your professional philosophy. Outline some of the basic tenets of your profession. Appropriate comments (but not necessarily required) may be: your personal beliefs about your work, your professional goals for the future, your outlook on populations typically served by your profession.

 

Adult education class. Attend and evaluate a seminar or class that addresses adult learners who are not receiving college credit for their attendance. Report on these aspects of the experience: program or content, effectiveness in achieving goals for the intended audience, appeal to adult learners, evaluation of the instructional techniques.

 

3-part Plan. Plan a 2-hour adult education seminar. 1) Prepare a detailed outline of the seminar, including logistics for it. 2) Prepare a single piece of publicity for it. 3) Present an excerpt of the seminar for the class.

 

Grading: Weight of assignments will be discussed in class. Final grades follow a standard scale:

A = 90%, B = 80%, C = 70%, D = 60%
