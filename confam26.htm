A dichotomy is always dangerous, but for the sake of contrast we will employ one here to discuss the “state of the family.”

Normative-adaptive 

Changes in family composition, including divorce, reflect societal changes that demand response by families and individuals.

People adapt to the larger pressures in many ways. Just because a change breaks with tradition, it need not be labeled wrong or pathological.

 Family decline 

Changes in families, including divorce, reflect the growing self-centeredness of Americans.

Largely a matter of lifestyle choices, the range of family types does not bode well for the future.

Many of our social issues are born of this family decline. Most can be repaired by the shoring up of family, starting with a commitment to traditional two-parent composition.
