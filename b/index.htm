B is for Boyer

Our faculty group experimented in casting our self-reviews in terms of the Boyer Model in electronic portfolio format. We created an ePortfolio program in TaskStream utilizing the materials of Drs. McGillin and Barbour. The TaskStream platform includes resources for creating the ePortfolio and we also created a scoring rubric.
 
Hyperlinks below provide access to portfolios by Dr. Joyce Armstrong, Dr. Mary Bold, and Dr. Lillian Chenoweth. The additional links are for a resource guide about creating the ePortfolio and a web-based view of the Directed Response Folio (DRF), which is TaskStream's term for a program that provides for reviewing and evaluating ePortfolios.
 
We see value in developing standards for such ePortfolios over time and we think our first effort points us in the right direction. As examples of the Boyer Model, our works are preliminary and incomplete. Our time was necessarily divided between working on content and scaling a new technology learning curve. Our expectation is to return to the Boyer-style narratives repeatedly in order to fully develop them. We aim to explore how such faculty portfolios can be useful for our own self-evaluation, for sharing with stakeholders, and for modeling scholarship for our students
