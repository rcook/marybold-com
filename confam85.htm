Statistics across the 20th century

  • From 1900 to 1998:

    1900 1998
    Population 76 million 270 million
    Currently divorced <200,000 19.4 million
    Married women in PLF <800,000 33.9 million
    Gas-fueled vehicles 8,000 208 million

  • The average household in 1900 had 4.8 people, and in 1998 it was 2.6 people.

  • In 1900, 11% of all 14- to 17-year-olds were enrolled in high school; in 1997, 93% were.

  • In 1900, about 95,000 people graduated from high school and 28,700 earned bachelor’s degrees. In 1997, 2.7 million people received high school diplomas and 1.2 million were awarded bachelor’s degrees.

  • Life expectancy increased from 46 years for men and 48 for women in 1900 to 74 years for men and 79 for women in 1997.

  • Influenza and pneumonia killed 202 out of every 100,000 persons in 1900; by 1997, the rate had fallen to 33.

  • There were 36 highway traffic fatalities in 1900 compared with 41,967 in 1997.

  • The 1920 census (first available data) showed that 35% of households had telephone service. This reached 94% in 1997.

  • In 1930 (again, first time data collected), 4 in 10 households had a radio. Since 1970, nearly all households had at least one radio.

  • In 1900, there were 5.7 million farms encompassing 841 million acres; in 1998 there were 2.2 million farms covering 954 million acres.
