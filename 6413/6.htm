Classic Books
reviewed in JMF in 2000

As Milardo mentions in the preface to Decade in Review, his interest in publishing in JMF in 2000 was not limited to the 1990s. Drawing from a survey of a large number of "classic" books of the century, he and invited scholars reviewed the following books as the most valuable books published. 

All Our Kin: Strategies for Survival in a Black Community. C. B. Stack. New York: Harper & Row. 1974.
Blue-Collar Marriage. M. Komarovsky. New York: Random House. 1964.
Children of the Great Depression. G. H. Elder. Chicago: University of Chicago Press. 1974.
Emerging Conceptual Frameworks in Family Analysis. F. I. Nye & F. M. Berardo (Eds.). New York: Macmillan. 1966, 1981.
Family and Social Network (2nd ed.). E. Bott. New York. Free Press. 1971.
Family, Socialization and Interaction Process. T. Parsons & R. F. Bales. Glencoe, IL: The Free Press. 1955.
The Future of Marriage. J. S. Bernard. New York: Bantam Books. 1972.
Husbands and Wives. Dynamics of Married Living. R. O. Blood & D. M. Wolfe. New York: The Free Press. 1960.
Inside the Family: Toward a Theory of Family Process. D. Kantor & W. Lehr. San Francisco: Jossey-Bass. 1975.
Middletown: A Study in Modern American Culture. R. S. Lynd & H. M. Lynd. New York: Harcourt, Brace. 1929.
Predicting Success or Failure in Marriage. E. W. Burgess & L. S. Cottrell, Jr. New York: Prentice-Hall. 1939.
The Social Psychology of Groups. J. W. Thibaut & H. H. Kelley. New York: Wiley, 1959.
