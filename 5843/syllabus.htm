Course Objectives:

  1. Compare theories of curriculum development and instruction.
  2. Determine concepts, objectives, content, and instructional strategies relevant to family and consumer sciences.
  3. Develop instructional techniques based on curriculum goals and learner characteristics, encompassing those associated with both andragogy and pedagogy.
  4. Demonstrate varied teaching and learning methods and technologies.
  5. Design appropriate needs assessment strategies.
  6. Design appropriate evaluation strategies.
  7. Evaluate educational and professional programs.
  8. Evaluate teaching and learning resources.
  9. Family & Consumer Sciences Post-baccalaureate Certification students: Develop applications appropriate to the Pedagogy and Professional Responsibilities (8-12) Standards.
    1. Standard I. The teacher designs instruction appropriate for all students that reflects an understanding of relevant content and is based on continuous and appropriate assessment.
    2. Standard II. The teacher creates a classroom environment of respect and rapport that fosters a positive climate for learning, equity, and excellence.
    3. Standard III. The teacher promotes student learning by providing responsive instruction that makes use of effective communication techniques, instructional strategies that actively engage students in the learning process, and timely, high-quality feedback.
    4. Standard IV. The teacher fulfills professional roles and responsibilities and adheres to legal and ethical requirements of the profession.
  10. Family & Consumer Sciences Post-baccalaureate Certification students: Develop applications appropriate to the Technology Applications Standards.
    1. Standard I. All teachers use technology-related terms, concepts, data input strategies, and ethical practices to make informed decisions about current technologies and their applications.
    2. Standard II. All teachers identify task requirements, apply search strategies, and use current technology to efficiently acquire, analyze, and evaluate a variety of electronic information.
    3. Standard III. All teachers use task-appropriate tools to synthesize knowledge, create and modify solutions, and evaluate results in a way that supports the work on individuals and groups in problem-solving situations.
    4. Standard IV. All teachers communicate information in different formats and for diverse audiences.
    5. Standard V. All teachers know how to plan, organize, deliver, and evaluate instruction for all students that incorporates the effective use of current technology for teaching and integrating the Technology Applications Texas Essential Knowledge and Skills (TEKS) into the curriculum.
  11. Family & Consumer Sciences Post-baccalaureate Certification students: Develop applications appropriate to the FCS Standards.
    1. Standard 1. The FCS teacher integrates foundational knowledge and skills across FCS content areas to prepare students for personal, family, community and career roles.
    2. Standard 2. The FCS teacher has comprehensive knowledge and skills of personal development, relationships, management to enhance quality of life and employment and entrepreneurial opportunities in family studies and human services.
    3. Standard 3. The FCS teacher has comprehensive knowledge and skills of child growth and development, parent/caregiver roles and responsibilities to enhance the child’s quality of life, and career applications.
    4. Standard 4. The FCS teacher has comprehensive knowledge and skills of food science, food technology, and nutrition and their relationship to growth, development, health and wellness to make informed choices, promote good health and pursue careers.
    5. Standard 5. The FCS teacher integrates knowledge, skills and practices for careers in hospitality, foods and institutional services.
    6. Standard 6. The FCS teacher understands consumer practices, processes and responsibilities that effect personal, family and work life.
    7. Standard 7. The FCS teacher has comprehensive knowledge of the textile and apparel industries which encompasses the production, marketing, and consumption of textiles and apparel products.
    8. Standard 8. The FCS teacher has comprehensive knowledge and skills related to interior and exterior environments, construction, and furnishings to make wise consumer decisions, increase productivity, and compete in industry.

 

Course Requirements:

 

  1. Contribute to the development of extended course materials for use by all class members. Total across the Maymester: 12 points.
  2. Develop and present to the class a workshop designed for presentation in community. (FCS Certification students should draw from the FCS Standards for topics.) Written: 15 points. Presentation: 15 points.
  3. Develop and discuss with the class a semester-long curriculum for use in one of these settings: K-12, lower division college course (includes A.A. degree level at community or junior college), upper division college course, graduate level course. Written: 20 points. Informal presentation: 10 points.
  4. Final exam conducted in last two hours of course (on site): 18 points
  5. Participation in class discussions and activities. Total across the Maymester: 10 points.
  6. If substituting this course for EDUC 5043, 15 hours of field experience in a family and consumer sciences classroom is required. In order to observe the required number of hours, you may use up to eight (8) hours of the required time to reflect on your own classroom teaching. If you are the only family and consumer sciences teacher in your school, ask your principal to suggest a master teacher in another teaching area who you can observe during your conference period. An observation log and reflective journal must be kept. The observation log should note the date and time of each observation and the teacher observed (include the same information for each of your own classes that you use for observation). Following each classroom visit, reflect on what you observed: what teaching or management strategies did you see? What specific learner characteristics did you observe? How did the environment enhance/deter learning? What did you learn from your visit that you will use or not use in the classroom? Failure to complete the field experience will result in failure of the class. Submit two copies of the log/journal.

Family Sciences Diversity Policy:

 

The concept of diversity implies a total commitment to an open context that promotes equality, social tolerance, and the right to express different points of view. The Family Sciences Department practices and celebrates diversity and creates a culture characterized by a climate of inclusion rather than exclusion, where people are involved in working together for a common good. Diversity refers to the inclusion of all persons regardless of their differences. These differences include but are not limited to ethnicity, race, culture, gender, age, socio-economic status, religious, sexual orientation, and physical disability. The Family Sciences Department does not condone discrimination in any form and complies with Texas Woman’s University Non-Discrimination Policy. The faculty actively seek enrichment through the strength, power, and wisdom of diversity.
