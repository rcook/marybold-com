Children (< 18) who have no usual source of health care
(emergency rooms excluded)

  • In 1996, 6.1% of children had no usual source of health care, according to their parents. This represents a decline from 7.6% in 1993.

  • In 1996, children with public insurance were almost twice as likely to have no usual source of care as children with private insurance. 

  • Children who were uninsured were over seven times as likely as those with private insurance to have no usual source of care in 1996.
