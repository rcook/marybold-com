Catalog Description:

Investigation of current trends, options, and opportunities in labor force for Family Sciences majors. Analysis of career development techniques and ethical issues. Emphasis on interaction of work and family. Development of professional portfolio. Prerequisite: Junior or senior standing. Three lecture hours a week. Credit: Three hours.

 

 

Course Objectives:

  1. Develop an awareness of options and opportunities for Family Sciences majors.
  2. Analyze the implications of career choices for society, the economy, the family, and the individual.
  3. Identify influences on career choices.
  4. Acquire career development strategies and techniques. 

 

 

Course Requirements:

  1. Class participation in discussions and activities. 50 points
  2. Current Issues: write summaries of 5 articles relating to job market trends, gender differences in pay, workplace benefits, work/home balance, and dual career/employment families. Each summary = 200 words minimum. Select your own due dates; three (3) summaries must be turned in before the mid-term exam. 50 points
  3. Job App Scavenger Hunt: join a team responsible for collecting job applications in a particular field. Present your findings in class; use the document camera to display some of the applications. 25 points
  4. Goals & Eco-map: develop a graphic or written account of your goals following graduation. Note your expected progress at 1 year, 3 years, and 5 years. Include an ecological model of both roadblocks to and supports for your goals. (Utilize Bronfenbrenner’s model to identify roadblocks and supports at the micro, meso, exo, and macro levels; these may also be described in terms of environments.) 50 points
  5. Career Services Assessments: complete assessments distributed in class (on September 13) and participate in analysis (on October 4). 50 points
  6. Career Fair: attend the TWU-sponsored career fair on September 27. Write a summary of your activities or of what you learned there. Length = 200 – 400 words. 50 points
  7. Portfolio: create a portfolio that reflects your accomplishments, talents, and career goals. These required elements of the portfolio will be submitted on specific dates: one-page resume, detailed multi-page resume, one-page keyword resume (customized for application for a specific job). Other elements will vary according to individual experience. 100 points
  8. Mid-term exam. 50 points
  9. Final exam. 75 points

Family Sciences Diversity Policy:

 

The concept of diversity implies a total commitment to an open context that promotes equality, social tolerance, and the right to express different points of view. The Family Sciences Department practices and celebrates diversity and creates a culture characterized by a climate of inclusion rather than exclusion, where people are involved in working together for a common good. Diversity refers to the inclusion of all persons regardless of their differences. These differences include but are not limited to ethnicity, race, culture, gender, age, socio-economic status, religion, sexual orientation, and physical disability. The Family Sciences Department does not condone discrimination in any form and complies with Texas Woman’s University Non-Discrimination Policy. The faculty actively seek enrichment through the strength, power, and wisdom of diversity.
