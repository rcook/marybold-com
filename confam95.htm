Percentage of children under age 18 by presence of
parents in household, 1980-98

  • In 1998, 68% of American children lived with two parents, down from 77% in 1980. 

  • In 1998, almost a quarter (23%) of children lived with only their mothers, 4% lived with only their fathers, and 4% lived with neither of their parents.

  • Since 1996, the percentage living with only one parent has not changed significantly. 

  • White, non-Hispanic children are much more likely than Black children and somewhat more likely than Hispanic children to live with two parents. In 1998, 76% of White, non-Hispanic children lived with two parents, compared to 36% of Black children and 64% of children of Hispanic origin.
