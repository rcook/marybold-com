Blended family has become a popular alternative name for stepfamily. (See also stepfamily.)

As large numbers of families experience divorce, remarriage, and blending of kin networks, the blended family now extends across several generations. (See also beanpole family composition.) Consequently, children may have three, four, or even more sets of grandparents. Sibling and cousin relationships may also be numerous, reminiscent of the large family sizes of previous generations.
