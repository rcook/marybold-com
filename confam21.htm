Fictive kin describes individuals or networks that function as family but who are not related by blood or marriage. Although frequently associated with African-Americans, fictive kin exist among all ethnic groups.

All of these relationships are examples of fictive kin:

  • elderly neighbors who are “our adopted grandparents”

  • “Aunt” Tess who used to live with cousin Frank

  • our church deacon who pays for my summer camp every year

  • Dad’s best friend who helped with my college costs

  • our neighbor who comes to all family events

  • my godparents who never forget my birthday
