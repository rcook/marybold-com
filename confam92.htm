Children (< 18) in poverty by family structure, 1980-97

  • In 1997, 19% of children lived in families with cash incomes below the poverty line. (Family of four with income < $16,400)

  • The percentage of children in poverty has stayed about 20% since 1981.

  • In 1997, 22% of children under age 6 lived in poverty, compared to 18% of older children. 

  • In 1997, 10% of children in married-couple families were living in poverty, compared to 49% in female-householder families. 

  • In 1997, 11% of White children lived in poverty, compared to 37% of Black children and 36% of Hispanic children. 

  • In 1997, 8% of all children lived in families with incomes less than half the poverty level, or $8,200 a year for a family of four.

  • 30% of children lived in families with incomes less than 150 percent of the poverty level, or $24,600 a year for a family of four.
