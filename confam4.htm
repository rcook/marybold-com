#1 Problem: Terms such as traditional … standard … normal … and ideal carry a powerful message of deficit model. So, “nuclear family” implies that if a family’s composition is different, then it is non-standard, abnormal, or less than ideal.

#2 Problem: “Nuclear” frequently makes an assumption about gender roles, including employment of women. Typically, this has been the “Ozzie and Harriet” model. (Of course, Harriet Nelson in real life was not an unemployed at-home mother.)

#3 Problem: Nuclear family with an at-home mother has never actually reflected the statistically typical American family. In 1940, 41% of American families could be labeled “Ozzie and Harriet.” In 1960, the figure was 43%. In short, the majority of American families has never matched the common image of nuclear family in terms of having an at-home mother.
