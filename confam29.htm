PLF = paid labor force
LFP = labor force participation

Designations such as PLF help in our discussions of employment for family members. Especially women have experienced labeling and stereotyping regarding their work status. “Working women” may refer to women working in the paid labor force—or just as accurately to women who maintain home or family without ever receiving a paycheck.

Using a term such as PLF helps to be specific: parents in PLF means the parents receive monetary compensation for their work (and that would not exclude self-employed individuals).

By the same token, “non-working” does not necessarily mean “no paycheck,” although for most of us it does refer to the parent at home. Unfortunately, the language serves to de-value work in the home.

Some informal language can help: at-home parent, at-home mom, at-home dad. Such terms can help clarify our meaning, at least until we learn what words the family prefers. For some people, “housewife,” “househusband,” and “homemaker” just don’t satisfy. And neither do “working woman” and “working mother.” Interestingly, we have not heard complaint from men about being labeled “working men” although many at-home fathers have been frustrated with the lack of language to describe their own choice of location.
