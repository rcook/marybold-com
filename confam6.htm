Can we be too liberal in framing family? Apparently not. American society is fairly brimming with a wide variety of family forms. (Some are detailed in the next section on Vocabulary.) As noted before, nuclear family composition has been an idealized but not typical form. 

But problems do emerge from our various meanings of family.

Problem #1: how do definitions translate to public policy? Who is a dependent when that word has monetary value? When does the state become a stakeholder in a family unit (in the case of foster families, for example)?

Problem #2: how are newer forms of family received in their communities?

Problem #3: by recognizing and supporting all forms of family, does society fail to encourage forms that promote the greater good?

Problem #4: discussion of family is downright cumbersome since one or multiple operational definitions may be required.
