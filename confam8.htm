In 1926, Ernest Burgess wrote this definition of family: 

a unity of interacting personalities

Journals published in the decade following reflect how popular Burgess and his definition were. Frequently referenced, the phrase became a common operational definition in research as well as a standard in essays and editorials. 

(Burgess taught sociology at the University of Chicago for 50 years until his death in 1966. His textbooks and writings of the 1920s and 1930s are credited with shaping the direction of sociology in the U.S. His research included the family as a social unit, marriage stability, and the prediction of marital success. Burgess is generally regarded as the founder of family studies.)
